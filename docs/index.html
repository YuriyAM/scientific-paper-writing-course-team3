Home

Write the paper as an evidence chain, not as disconnected sections.

This mock author-guidelines website translates a scientific writing manual into a formal, navigable resource for student authors. Its central idea is simple: the paper should move coherently from topic to problem, from problem to questions, from questions to method, and from method to results and discussion.

Core principle

A scientific paper is not a collection of independent sections. It is a chain of reasoning from problem identification to evidence-based interpretation.

Evidence chain

The paper logic at a glance

Each part of the manuscript should support the same research problem. If one section cannot be traced to the central argument, it probably does not belong in the paper.

Use this sequence to test coherence, traceability, and contribution before you start polishing language or formatting.

Quality criteria

What makes the chain strong

Coherence

All sections should serve the same research problem and questions.

Traceability

Each major conclusion should be traceable to methods, evidence, and references.

Contribution

The paper should make clear what it clarifies, tests, extends, applies, or explains.

Practical use When writing a new paragraph, ask: Which part of the evidence chain does this support?
How to use this site

A simple reading path for student authors

Begin on the structure page

Refine the topic, define the problem and gap, align objectives with questions, and establish the structure of the paper.

Move to the writing guidelines

Use the literature, methodology, results, discussion, and ethics sections to develop each part of the manuscript with discipline and clarity.

Finish with the checklist

Review focus, logic, visual discipline, references, ethics, and AI verification before submission.
